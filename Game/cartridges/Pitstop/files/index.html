How to Play

Post your unit base-to-base around the Niagara Region. Each leg is a three-beat sequence — AP → ENP → BSE — typed as real PowerLine commands. Fast, correct typing is your throttle; a miss bleeds the speed.

The Pit — 72123 Fleet

Your pit is 72123 — Fleet, the garage that keeps the trucks running. It is not a stop on the course and it is not drawn on the map: it sits off the circuit, on the run down to the Start / Finish line.

That is the only place it can be taken. You come past the line at the end of every lap, and that is your window — the console calls the pit as you approach so you know it is there before you're past it. Anywhere else on the course, you're driving on what you've got.

Pitting is preview only for now — the advisory tells you the pit is open, but tires, fuel and the SWAP command don't yet change the race. The consequence layer is authorized separately.

Race Type B — Checkpoint

Pick Type B · Checkpoint in Race Options and ENP stops being an invisible rule and becomes two gantries over the road: the OPEN banner starts the window, the CLOSE banner ends it. Between them, a hit zone is painted across the tarmac.

How fast you TYPE sets how hard the car pulls. How accurately you land Enter on the painted zone sets how LONG the boost runs — nail the paint and you get the longest one. Watch the BOOST readout for the grade.

While the boost is live, ◀ ▶ arrow keys steer. The field doesn't move over for you: swerve past them. Contact costs you the boost, some speed and some rubber.

Shift Change

At real shift-change times a radio call warns you a change is coming. After ENP, type LA to stay mobile — a board of units opens. Clear each one through its chain (type <CMD> <UNIT>) before the roster clock catches it, then BSE to finish the leg. The car never stops.

24-hour units (pre-dawn / evening) run the full chain RCAV → OD → SS → BSEH → CAV SS; 12-hour starts (mid-day) come up as SP and begin at SS. NOTE may be added any time after SS. Set Shift Change in Race Options (or NONE to skip). Earlier = harder.
